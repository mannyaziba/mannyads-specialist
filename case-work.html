Ad Disapproval: Diagnosis & Resolution
Case ADS-002 · Account Suspension — Suspicious Payment Activity
The Problem
Issue
Account suspended — ad serving stopped completely
Reported
Client noticed ads offline overnight, no warning email
Initial read
Likely billing mismatch or landing page policy flag
Investigation
  • Dashboard showed "Suspended: Suspicious Payment Activity"
  • Payment history — card expired 6 days prior, auto-renewal failed silently
  • Reviewed policy — confirmed billing integrity flag, not a content violation
  • Audited ad copy and landing page — no content violations found
  • Documented all findings before taking action
Action Taken
  • Updated payment method and cleared outstanding balance
  • Drafted appeal citing exact cause — expired card, not suspicious intent
  • Submitted with supporting documentation
"We have identified that the suspension was triggered by a payment method expiry resulting in a failed renewal. This was not indicative of suspicious activity. We have updated payment details and cleared all outstanding balances. We respectfully request reinstatement of the account."
Outcome
Result
Account reinstated within 36 hours
Lesson
Billing suspensions resolve faster when the appeal names the exact policy and eliminates intent concerns
02 /
Appeal Writing Sample
Case ADS-001 · Disapproval — Misleading or Exaggerated Claims Policy
Context
  • Ad headline contained "guaranteed results" — flagged under Misleading Claims policy
  • Landing page made no such guarantee — headline was the sole trigger
  • Headline rewritten before appeal submitted
Appeal Written
SUBJECT: APPEAL — AD DISAPPROVAL / MISLEADING CLAIM

Thank you for reviewing this case. We have carefully reviewed the disapproval notice and identified that the flagged content was the ad headline, which contained the phrase "guaranteed results." We understand this falls under Google's Misleading or Exaggerated Claims policy, and we accept that this language was inappropriate.

We have updated the headline to accurately reflect our offering without making performance guarantees. The updated ad and landing page are now consistent with each other and fully compliant with Google's advertising policies.

We respectfully request that the updated ad be reviewed and approved. We are committed to maintaining policy compliance in all future campaigns. Thank you for your time.

Why This Works
  • Acknowledges the violation directly — no defensiveness
  • Names the specific policy — shows research was done
  • States the fix clearly — gives the reviewer confidence
  • Calm, professional tone — no frustration or urgency
  • Concise — easy for the reviewer to act on

Personal Appeals Framework
My standard process for every case — repeatable and documented
01
Identify
Read the notice in full. Identify the exact policy cited. Do not assume. Log the case immediately.
02
Investigate
Cross-reference the policy with ad content, landing page, and account history. Find the real cause.
03
Fix First
Fix the root cause before writing the appeal. Appealing without fixing first is a wasted submission.
04
Write the Appeal
Reference the specific policy by name. Describe the fix. Calm, concise, professional tone.
05
Submit & Track
Submit through the correct channel. Log in the tracker. Set a 48–72h follow-up reminder.
06
Follow Up
If unsuccessful, analyse the rejection, escalate if needed, adjust and resubmit with documentation.
